How the market-reference prices were derived

Methodology note — for the Registrar’s record

The market-reference price against every line has been normalised to the scale of Vikram Dev University (seventy-five affiliated colleges; approximately one and a half to two lakh students). The principal anchor contracts are reproduced below, all from the public record.

  • Saurashtra University — Mindlogicx: ₹72 Cr over six years for a 320-college end-to-end examination and ERP programme.
  • Alagappa University — TANII (Tamil Nadu): ₹5 Cr for examination automation plus ₹4.5 Cr for digital evaluation; that is ₹9.5 Cr on the examination domain alone.
  • Mindlogicx — AKTU (UP) and VTU (Karnataka): multi-crore multi-year deals for pre- and post-examination automation with a Network Operations Centre.
  • TCS iON client base (Andhra University, Kazi Nazrul University, CSVTU, KNRUHS, APSCHE): ₹5–25 Cr is the typical band for multi-year managed services.
  • MasterSoft UAIMS: published per-module rates for affiliated-college universities at comparable scale.
  • Samarth eGov (Ministry of Education): baseline cloud and customisation cost of ₹3–5 lakh per annum per institution, disclosed by the Joint Director, Samarth, in review minutes.

Where a comparable contract covered a wider scope, the figure has been apportioned to the module in question on a transparent basis (per-college, per-student, or per-module). Where no direct comparable exists — typically for the AI overlays — the reference has been set by triangulating the two closest Tier-1 systems-integrator quotations available to the atelier at the time of writing. Every figure in this paper is therefore traceable to either a named contract or a named comparable.

Why the atelier is structurally cheaper than the market

Cost structure

The 15 to 20 per cent saving is not a discount, and it is not a price drop taken merely to secure the engagement. It flows from three reasons that the University can verify independently.

One, no systems-integrator overhead. Kinyoubi Atelier & Co. is a lean, founder-led engineering operation. The firm carries none of the sales-force, delivery-hierarchy, and corporate-overhead load that a Tier-1 systems integrator builds into every line item. That overhead, conservatively, is twenty to thirty paise in every rupee; the saving is returned to the customer.

Two, Vikram Dev University is the flagship reference deployment. The atelier retains platform intellectual property and the right to re-licence onward to peer universities; that makes the University’s acquisition, in effect, cross-subsidised by future installations. This is disclosed, not hidden.

Three, proximity to Jeypore. The atelier’s engineering presence in the region means that travel, per-diem, and on-site time — the same heads that a Bengaluru or Gurugram integrator would normally bill to the University at full rate with a handling margin on top — are structurally absent. On-site visits during the build phase are included at no additional cost to the University.

The University therefore receives enterprise-grade build quality, perpetual use rights, and source-code escrow at the price point set out above. This is a deliberately committed commercial posture. It is audit-worthy on every front.
